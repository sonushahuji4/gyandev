Pattern-first DSA preparation for FAANG interviews. Each topic starts with a short theory chapter that codifies the mental models and shared tricks, then breaks the topic into one chapter per pattern — every pattern with a problem statement, intuition, visual walkthrough, dual-language solutions, complexity analysis, and an edge-case checklist.

The Intervals topic covers foundational concepts plus twenty-two problems. The core eleven — Merge Intervals, Insert Interval, Meeting Rooms I & II, Non-Overlapping Intervals, Interval List Intersections, Employee Free Time, Remove Interval, My Calendar I & II, and Data Stream as Disjoint Intervals — followed by eleven advanced extensions: Sweep Line Template, Min Arrows to Burst Balloons, My Calendar III, Car Pooling, Range Module, Meeting Rooms III, Difference Array, The Skyline Problem, Rectangle Area II, Interval Tree Template, and Task Scheduler.
